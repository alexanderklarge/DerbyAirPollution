Information About The Data

  • The data is from the DEFRA (Department for Environment Food & Rural Affairs) Automatic Urban and Rural Network (AURN) map
  • The sensor takes hourly readings, and the DEFRA website updates with a new .csv file at the end of every day
  • The sensor measures levels of Nitric Oxide and Nitrogen Dioxide. Other sensors also measure methane, PM2.5s etc, but sadly the Derby sensor doesn't.
  • The sensor has been operational since the 22nd of January 2017
  • Python code to clean the raw data and output various graphs (the GitHub repo of code used to make the below graphs & this site can be found here)

Weekly 2020 Pollution vs Mean and Median of Previous Years

The below 2 graphs show the total weekly pollution of 2020 compared to the mean (average) and median (middle, more reliable than the mean) of the previous 3 years of data, to help compare 2020 to what is "normal".

Total Weekly Pollution, Comparing Each Year

The below graphs show all 4 years worth of data plotted together. This is useful as you can see the value for each year, but it is much harder to assess general trends, which is why graphs 1 and 2 are more useful at-a-glance.

2020 Weekly Total Pollution As % of Median of Previous Years

If we take the median value of each week as the "normal" value for pollution for that week, we can then compare the 2020 value and see whether it is higher or lower than "normal".

Hourly Pollution In The 4 Week Window Immediately After Lockdown

The lockdown was officially announced on the 23rd of March, 2020. It is useful to compare the 4-week window from the lockdown (23/3/2020 - 19/4/2020) to the same window of previous years, to get a basic idea of the effect of the lockdown.

Monthly 2020 Pollution VS Median Of Previous Years
